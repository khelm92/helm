Recipe for Shepherd's Pie

Ingredients Cooking Steps Results

The best Shepherd's Pie I ever did taste was made by my girlfriend. In the following article, I will share with you the top secret homemade recipe, so you can also enjoy the best Shepherd's Pie ever. First off, let's make a list of what you will need to create this deliciousness.

Ingredients:

  • Ground Beef
  • Green Beans
  • Tomato Paste
  • Brown Potatoes
  • Cheddar Cheese
  • An oven-safe Casserole Pan

Now, let's get to the hard part, actually making it.

  1. Cook the ground beef on medium heat in a skillet until fully cooked. Drain and Set aside.
  2. Wash, peel, and chop down the taters.
  3. Boil the taters. Then mash the taters (add butter, milk, salt, pepper to taste). Set aside.
  4. Get your Casserole Pan, and put a layer of mashed taters (about half of them) at the bottom.
  5. Put all of the ground beef in one layer on top of the taters.
  6. Get your green beans, and put them in a layer on top of the ground beef.
  7. Put the tomato paste in another layer on top of the green beans.
  8. Put the other half of the taters on top of the green beans.
  9. Sprinkle the cheddar cheese on top of the taters.
  10. Preheat oven to 350 degrees, cook for 35-40 minutes. Let cool for 5 minutes. Bada bing, bada boom.

All About Cats

Everybody loves cats (if you don't, there's probably something wrong with you), so I'm making this page all about cats. Throughout this page you will find some pictures of awesome cats, interesting facts about cats, and just general information.

  • General Cat Information
  • Interesting Facts
  • Pictures

General Cat Information:

There are about 40 different breeds of cats, and they are the most popular pet in America with over 30% of households owning a cat. Not all countries have the same love for cats though (at least as pets), over 4 millions cats are eaten each year in Asian countries. The earliest known domesticated pet cat was found buried with it's owner in a 9,500-year-old grave on the Mediterranean island of Cyprus.

Interesting Facts:

  • The average domestic cat spends about 2/3 of it's life sleeping.
  • Cats sweat through their paws.
  • In the 1750s, Europeans introduced cats into the Americas to control pests.
  • A commemorative tower was built in Scotland for a cat named Towser, who caught nearly 30,000 mice in her lifetime.
  • A cat almost never meows at another cat, mostly just humans. Cats typically will spit, purr, and hiss at other cats.
  • A cats hearing is better than a dogs. And a cat can hear high-frequency sounds up to two octaves higher than a human.
  • A cat can jump up two five times it's own height.
  • Cats rub their faces against people to mark their territory with scent glands around their mouth.
